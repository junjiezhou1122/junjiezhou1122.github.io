About

Builder, researcher, and systems thinker.

Most of my work lives between knowledge workflows, agentic tools, and learning products. I care about systems that do more than store information — they should help people notice patterns, organize context, and use what they know at the moment it matters.

That means moving between product direction, interface design, research synthesis, and implementation. I’m especially interested in turning messy inputs — papers, bookmarks, writing traces, questions, and project logs — into structures that support long term learning and better judgment.

Principles

What I keep returning to.

  1. 01

    Learning should become usable.

    I care less about accumulation than conversion. Notes, ideas, and inputs matter when they become action, practice, or better judgment.

  2. 02

    AI should work from context, not generic prompts.

    The most helpful systems understand the user’s material, history, and current direction instead of pretending every task starts from zero.

  3. 03

    Good tools reduce friction without flattening nuance.

    The point is not to automate away thinking. It is to make the right kind of thinking easier to sustain.

  4. 04

    Research matters more when it becomes a workflow.

    I’m drawn to ideas that can cross the boundary from paper, note, or prototype into something people can actually use.
